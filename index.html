In a tower high, where the ravens croon, Lies the jester’s fate, under the wane of moon. Cursed by the spin of a witch’s loom, Doomed to dwell in a “crazy” room. For centuries old, in a cap and bells, My laughter echoes through these cells. A quest bestowed, by ancient rune, To seek and gather under the sun and moon. A myriad of objects, scattered wide, In hidden corners where shadows bide. From dragon's tooth to the witch's spoon, A list unending, like a haunting tune. But luck, she dances just out of reach, A cruel mistress, with much to teach. Each trinket found, in night or noon, Just slips away, like a fleeting boon. For freedom’s key, in jest’s attire, Is not just luck, but hearts afire. The final jest, I’ve yet to croon, Will break the spell, end the cursed tune. So gather 'round, ye brave and plucky, Join my quest, perhaps we'll be lucky. Through riddle and jest, we chase our doom, In hopes to escape this living tomb.
